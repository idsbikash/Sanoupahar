The Reality in Nepal

Millions of Nepalis struggle daily for basic necessities. These are not statistics — they are lives.

🍚

Food Insecurity

Over 4 million people in Nepal face moderate to severe food insecurity. Children in remote areas often go to bed hungry.

🧣

Lack of Clothing

In winter, many families cannot afford warm clothes. Children walk to school without shoes or jackets.

🚻

Sanitation Crisis

Nearly 8 million people lack access to basic sanitation. Open defecation and unsafe water cause preventable diseases.

🏚️

Shelter & Dignity

Thousands of elderly and orphans live in shelters with minimal resources. They need more than just food — they need care.

What People Face Every Day

These are the problems we work to solve — not through charity, but through connection and action.

Hunger & Nutrition

42% of children under 5 are stunted

Chronic malnutrition affects physical and cognitive development. Many families survive on one meal a day.

Clothing & Warmth

60% of rural households lack winter clothing

In high-altitude regions, children and elders suffer from cold-related illnesses because they cannot afford warm layers.

Sanitation & Hygiene

30% of schools lack proper toilets

Girls often drop out when they reach puberty due to lack of safe, private sanitation facilities.

Isolation of Elderly & Orphans

Over 100,000 children live in orphanages

Many of these children have living parents but are placed in institutions due to poverty. Old-age homes are underfunded.

Stories from the Ground

Real voices from Nepal's villages and cities.

🍲 "We have rice, but no vegetables."

— A mother in Jumla

Her family grows enough rice for three months, but for the rest of the year they rely on wild greens and whatever they can forage. Their children often go to school without lunch.

"If someone could send just a little oil and lentils, it would make such a difference."

🧣 "We wear the same sweater all winter."

— A grandfather in Dolpa

He and his grandson share one thin blanket during the harsh Himalayan winter. Many families in his village have no warm clothes for their children.

"All we need is something to keep the cold away."
